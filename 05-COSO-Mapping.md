## Detailed COSO Principle Mapping

### Component 1: Control Environment (Principles 1–5)

#### Principle 1 — Demonstrates Commitment to Integrity and Ethical Values

| Status | **Partially Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | F01, F02, F04, F12, F13 |
| **Analysis** | OmniCare has a corporate code of conduct but no IT-specific ethical standards or governance code. The absence of an IT governance framework (F01) and steering committee (F02) means IT integrity standards are inconsistently applied. Fragmented IT decisions (F04) create opportunities for control bypass. The lack of an internal control framework (F12) and compliance program (F13) further erodes the control environment. |
| **Gap** | No IT governance charter, no IT code of ethics, no defined IT values or expected behaviours. |

#### Principle 2 — Board Exercises Oversight Responsibilities

| Status | **Partially Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | F01, F02, F03, F12 |
| **Analysis** | The board receives financial reporting but does not exercise structured IT oversight. No IT steering committee reports to the board. The board has not approved an IT strategy (F03) or governance framework (F01). IT-related internal controls (F12) lack board-level visibility. |
| **Gap** | No IT governance reporting to the board. No board-level IT committee. IT agenda items are not a standing board meeting component. |

#### Principle 3 — Establishes Structure, Authority, and Responsibility

| Status | **Partially Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | F01, F04, F10, F15 |
| **Analysis** | IT organisational structures exist but are inconsistent across acquisitions. Authority for IT decisions is undefined (F01, F04). IT roles lack standardised responsibility definitions (F10). Resource allocation authority is fragmented (F15). |
| **Gap** | No enterprise-wide IT organisation chart. IT authority matrix not documented. Role duplication and gaps exist across entities. |

#### Principle 4 — Demonstrates Commitment to Competence

| Status | **Minimally Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | F10 |
| **Analysis** | IT competency requirements are not formally defined. Training is ad-hoc. The absence of a competency framework (F10) directly impacts this principle. High turnover in IT indicates competence development gaps. |
| **Gap** | No IT competency framework. No structured training or certification programs. Career development paths are undefined. |

#### Principle 5 — Enforces Accountability

| Status | **Not Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | None |
| **Analysis** | There are no formal accountability mechanisms for IT governance, performance, or risk management. No performance evaluations linked to IT governance responsibilities. No consequences for governance failures. |
| **Gap** | No accountability framework. IT governance responsibilities not in job descriptions. No performance metrics linked to governance outcomes. |

### Component 2: Risk Assessment (Principles 6–9)

#### Principle 6 — Specifies Objectives with Sufficient Clarity

| Status | **Minimally Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | F05 |
| **Analysis** | IT objectives are not formally defined or communicated in a way that enables risk identification. The absence of an IT risk management process (F05) means risk assessment cannot be properly conducted. |
| **Gap** | No defined IT objectives hierarchy. No risk appetite thresholds. Operational and compliance objectives not linked to risk assessment. |

#### Principle 7 — Identifies and Analyses Risks

| Status | **Partially Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | F05, F07, F08 |
| **Analysis** | IT risk identification is limited to cybersecurity threats (F08). Post-merger integration risks (F07) are not formally assessed. No structured risk analysis methodology exists (F05). |
| **Gap** | No risk register. No risk analysis methodology. Risk identification is incomplete, focusing primarily on security threats. |

#### Principle 8 — Considers Potential for Fraud

| Status | **Not Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | None |
| **Analysis** | Fraud risk assessment is not integrated with IT governance. No IT fraud risk assessment has been conducted. Segregation of duties controls are not documented. |
| **Gap** | No fraud risk assessment for IT processes. No IT fraud detection controls. |

#### Principle 9 — Identifies and Assesses Changes

| Status | **Not Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | None |
| **Analysis** | Organisational, regulatory, and technology changes are not systematically assessed for their impact on internal control. The rapid acquisition and growth context makes this a significant gap. |
| **Gap** | No change impact assessment process for internal control. Regulatory change monitoring is manual and incomplete. |

---

### Component 3: Control Activities (Principles 10–12)

#### Principle 10 — Selects and Develops Control Activities

| Status | **Partially Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | F06, F09, F12, F14, F15 |
| **Analysis** | Control activities are inconsistently applied. Project governance controls (F06), enterprise architecture standards (F09), and IT operations controls (F14) are weak. The absence of an internal control framework (F12) means control activities are not systematically selected or designed. Resource optimisation controls (F15) are absent. |
| **Gap** | Control activities are not mapped to objectives. No control selection methodology. Preventive and detective controls are unbalanced. |

#### Principle 11 — Selects and Develops General Controls over Technology

| Status | **Partially Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | F07, F08, F12, F14 |
| **Analysis** | IT general controls (ITGCs) are not documented or tested (F12). Security controls are basic (F08) and not standardised across entities. Operations controls (F14) are informal. Post-merger technology integration (F07) has introduced control gaps. |
| **Gap** | ITGCs (access, change, operations, program development) are undocumented. No control testing programme. |

#### Principle 12 — Deploys Controls through Policies and Procedures

| Status | **Minimally Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | F08, F12 |
| **Analysis** | IT policies exist in some areas (F08) but are inconsistent across entities. Policy management is decentralised. No policy lifecycle management process. The internal control framework (F12) is absent, meaning control deployment lacks structure. |
| **Gap** | Inconsistent policy framework. No central policy repository. No policy review or approval process. |

---

### Component 4: Information and Communication (Principles 13–15)

#### Principle 13 — Uses Relevant Information

| Status | **Minimally Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | F03, F09 |
| **Analysis** | IT strategy information (F03) and architecture information (F09) are not systematically captured or used for decision-making. No IT performance data is reliably produced (F11). |
| **Gap** | No IT information model. Decision-relevant IT data is not identified, captured, or communicated. |

#### Principle 14 — Communicates Internally

| Status | **Not Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | None |
| **Analysis** | There are no structured IT communication channels. IT governance expectations, policies, and performance are not communicated to employees. No formal IT newsletters, intranet presence, or town halls. |
| **Gap** | No IT communication plan. Internal IT communication is informal and inconsistent. |

#### Principle 15 — Communicates Externally

| Status | **Not Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | None |
| **Analysis** | External IT communication (to customers, regulators, auditors) is handled ad-hoc. No formal external communication policy for IT matters. |
| **Gap** | No external IT communication policy. Regulatory communication is reactive. |

---

### Component 5: Monitoring Activities (Principles 16–17)

#### Principle 16 — Conducts Ongoing and/or Separate Evaluations

| Status | **Partially Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | F02, F06, F11 |
| **Analysis** | No ongoing or periodic evaluation of IT internal controls. The absence of an IT steering committee (F02) means no governance oversight body conducts evaluations. Benefits realisation reviews (F06) are absent. Performance monitoring (F11) is not conducted. |
| **Gap** | No internal control evaluation programme. No IT audit function. Evaluation scope and frequency are undefined. |

#### Principle 17 — Evaluates and Communicates Deficiencies

| Status | **Minimally Addressed** |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | F11, F13 |
| **Analysis** | IT deficiencies are not systematically identified or communicated. Compliance deficiencies (F13) are tracked informally. Performance deficiencies (F11) are not captured due to lack of monitoring. No deficiency escalation process exists. |
| **Gap** | No deficiency management process. No escalation criteria for IT control deficiencies. |
