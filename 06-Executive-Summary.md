## Assessment Overview

This report presents the findings of a comprehensive IT governance gap assessment conducted at OmniCare Health during Q2 2026. The assessment evaluated **19 COBIT 2019 governance and management objectives** across five domains, mapped each finding to **COSO Internal Control вҖ” Integrated Framework (2013) principles**, and developed a prioritized remediation roadmap.

### Why This Assessment Was Undertaken

OmniCare Health has grown from a focused telemedicine provider to a $200M healthcare technology enterprise through three acquisitions in 24 months. This rapid scaling has introduced:

- Fragmented IT decision-making across business units
- Redundant technology investments (3 EHR platforms, 2 identity systems)
- No IT steering committee or governance oversight
- Undocumented IT general controls вҖ” a material weakness risk for SOX readiness
- Ad-hoc risk management exposed by a 2025 ransomware incident

**The board and executive leadership commissioned this assessment to baseline current governance maturity and build a roadmap to achieve SOX readiness and enterprise risk management maturity.**

## Key Strengths

While the overall maturity is low, the assessment identified several areas OmniCare can build upon:

1. **Executive awareness and commitment** вҖ” Leadership recognizes the governance gap and has sponsored this assessment. The CEO, CFO, and CTO are aligned on the need for change.
2. **Technical engineering capability** вҖ” The engineering team follows sound development practices (CI/CD, code review). BAI03 scored at Level 2, the highest in the BAI domain.
3. **Basic security foundation** вҖ” A CISO and security team exist with fundamental controls deployed (antivirus, firewall, MFA). This provides a base to build a comprehensive security program.
4. **Financial discipline** вҖ” IT budget management, while siloed, is practiced at the departmental level. This indicates capability to adopt enterprise-level resource management.
5. **Regulatory awareness** вҖ” The compliance officer has identified key obligations. The function needs resourcing but the awareness exists.

## Conclusion

OmniCare Health's IT governance maturity of **1.37/5.0** is characteristic of a rapidly scaled organization that has outgrown its informal governance model. The gap to the target of **3.16** is significant but achievable within **12вҖ“18 months** with executive commitment and a structured investment of **$1.6MвҖ“$2.5M**.

The four critical-risk findings вҖ” absence of governance framework, no steering committee, no risk management process, and inadequate security governance вҖ” must be addressed as a priority. These foundational gaps underpin all other deficiencies and are prerequisites for SOX readiness.

Critically, the recommended investment of **$1.6MвҖ“$2.5M** is expected to deliver **$4.1MвҖ“$6.8M** in annual benefits through cost optimization (reducing IT spend from 5.2% to 3.8% of revenue), risk reduction (lower insurance premiums, avoided incidents), and SOX compliance (avoiding material weakness findings).

**The cost of inaction** вҖ” continued regulatory exposure, fragmented IT costs, inability to achieve SOX compliance, and elevated security risk вҖ” far exceeds the investment required to reach a defined, standardized governance state.
