## 1. Engagement Context

### 1.1 Business Drivers

OmniCare Health has experienced rapid expansion through three acquisitions over the past 24 months. While this growth has accelerated revenue to $200M and expanded the customer base to 400+ healthcare providers, it has also introduced significant governance fragmentation:

- **Decentralized IT decisions** — Business units independently procure and manage technology, resulting in redundant systems and inconsistent security postures.
- **No IT steering committee** — IT investments lack centralized prioritization, leading to cost inefficiencies and strategic misalignment.
- **Post-merger integration debt** — Acquired entities operate on legacy platforms with no standardized integration roadmap.
- **SOX readiness pressure** — As OmniCare approaches $200M revenue, external auditors and the board have flagged the need for formal internal controls over financial reporting (ICFR).
- **Regulatory exposure** — Healthcare data regulations (HIPAA, HITECH) demand demonstrable governance of IT-related risks.

### 1.2 Assessment Objectives

| Objective | Description |
|---|---|
| **Baseline maturity** | Establish current capability levels across COBIT 2019 governance and management objectives |
| **Gap identification** | Identify specific deficiencies between current and target maturity states |
| **Risk prioritization** | Rank gaps by business impact, regulatory exposure, and strategic importance |
| **COSO alignment** | Map each gap to COSO 2013 principles to support integrated reporting |
| **Roadmap development** | Deliver actionable recommendations with timeframes, effort estimates, and maturity uplift projections |

## 2. Assessment Scope

### 2.1 In-Scope

- **Organizational units:** Corporate IT, Information Security, Engineering, Product Management, Finance/Compliance, and all three acquired subsidiaries
- **Processes:** IT governance structure, strategic planning, risk management, security management, project/programme management, IT operations, vendor management, compliance monitoring
- **Systems:** Telemedicine platform, patient data management system, EHR integration layer, corporate ERP/CRM, identity management infrastructure
- **Regulatory:** SOX readiness, HIPAA/HITECH compliance, GDPR (EU patient data)

### 2.2 Out-of-Scope

- Detailed technical vulnerability assessments
- Third-party vendor security audits
- Financial statement audit procedures
- Physical security controls at data centers
- Business continuity / disaster recovery technical testing

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Maturity Model (COBIT 2019 Capability Levels)

| Level | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **0** | Incomplete | No processes exist; governance/management activities not performed |
| **1** | Performed (Ad-hoc) | Processes are performed intuitively on a case-by-case basis; no formal documentation |
| **2** | Managed (Basic) | Processes are planned, monitored, and repeatable at the operational level |
| **3** | Defined (Standardized) | Processes are standardized, documented, and integrated across the enterprise |
| **4** | Measured (Quantitative) | Processes are quantitatively managed with defined metrics and KPIs |
| **5** | Optimizing (Continuous) | Processes are continuously improved using quantitative feedback and innovation |

### 3.2 Assessment Approach

```
┌────────────┐     ┌────────────┐     ┌─────────────┐     ┌──────────────┐
│  Planning  │────►│  Evidence   │────►│  Analysis & │────►│  Reporting   │
│ & Scoping  │     │ Gathering   │     │  Rating     │     │  & Roadmap   │
└────────────┘     └────────────┘     └─────────────┘     └──────────────┘
```

**Phase 1 — Planning (Week 1):** Kick-off meeting, document request list, stakeholder identification, schedule interviews.

**Phase 2 — Evidence Gathering (Weeks 2–4):** Semi-structured interviews with 15+ stakeholders; review of existing policies, procedures, org charts, meeting minutes, project artifacts; process walkthroughs for key domains (change management, incident response, procurement).

**Phase 3 — Analysis and Rating (Weeks 5–6):** Evidence consolidated into objective-by-objective maturity assessments; gap calculations; risk ratings assigned using a 3×3 impact-likelihood matrix.

**Phase 4 — Reporting and Roadmap (Week 7):** Gap analysis report, prioritized recommendations, COSO mapping matrix, executive summary and board presentation.

### 3.3 Rating Guidelines

- Maturity is assessed based on **demonstrable evidence**, not self-attestation.
- Where evidence is contradictory (e.g., a policy exists but is not followed), the lower maturity prevails.
- Each objective is rated independently; aggregated scores are arithmetic means.
- Target maturity is set collaboratively with executive sponsors based on business risk appetite and industry benchmarks (healthcare technology peers).

## 4. Data Gathering Approach

### 4.1 Evidence Sources

| Source Type | Count | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Executive interviews | 6 | CEO, CFO, CTO, CPO, VP Engineering, VP Product |
| Process owner interviews | 10 | IT Operations, Security, Compliance, HR, each BU lead |
| Document review | 25+ | Existing policies, org charts, RACI matrices, project charters, board decks |
| Process walkthroughs | 8 | Incident management, change management, procurement, vendor onboarding, access control, project governance, budget planning, compliance reporting |
| System demonstrations | 4 | Service desk platform, ERP, identity management, monitoring tools |
| Acquirer legacy documentation | 3 sets | Pre-merger policies and process documentation from acquired entities |

### 4.2 Interview Protocol

All interviews follow a structured protocol aligned to the COBIT 2019 assessment guide:

1. Confirm process scope and boundaries
2. Walk through current process flow (as-is)
3. Identify governance artifacts (policies, procedures, controls)
4. Discuss performance metrics and monitoring
5. Assess awareness and training
6. Collect evidence references
7. Rate maturity using predefined indicators
