## 2. EDM — Evaluate, Direct and Monitor (Governance)

### EDM01 — Ensure Governance Framework Setting and Maintenance

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|
| **Current Maturity** | **1** — Performed (Ad-hoc) |
| **Target Maturity** | **3** — Defined (Standardized) |
| **Gap** | **2** |
| **Evidence** | No formal governance framework exists. Board-level IT oversight is absent. No IT governance charter or policy framework. IT decision rights are undefined. Acquired entities follow their own legacy governance models. |
| **Key Observations** | The CEO acts as de-facto IT decision-maker without a structured forum. No RACI matrix exists for IT decisions. Governance artefacts from acquisitions have not been consolidated. |

### EDM02 — Ensure Benefits Delivery

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|
| **Current Maturity** | **1** — Performed (Ad-hoc) |
| **Target Maturity** | **3** — Defined (Standardized) |
| **Gap** | **2** |
| **Evidence** | No benefits realization process exists. IT investments are approved without formal business cases, measurable KPIs, or post-implementation reviews. Value tracking is limited to budget variance. |
| **Key Observations** | Three major platform investments in the past year have no documented benefits tracking. Stakeholders report uncertainty about whether acquired platform consolidation delivered expected ROI. |

### EDM03 — Ensure Risk Optimisation

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|
| **Current Maturity** | **1** — Performed (Ad-hoc) |
| **Target Maturity** | **3** — Defined (Standardized) |
| **Gap** | **2** |
| **Evidence** | No enterprise IT risk management process. Risk discussions occur informally in executive meetings. No risk register, risk appetite statement, or risk treatment plan for IT. |
| **Key Observations** | A 2025 ransomware incident on an acquired legacy system was managed reactively without a formal incident response plan. HIPAA compliance risk is not formally assessed. Board has not defined IT risk appetite. |

### EDM04 — Ensure Resource Optimisation

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|
| **Current Maturity** | **2** — Managed (Basic) |
| **Target Maturity** | **3** — Defined (Standardized) |
| **Gap** | **1** |
| **Evidence** | IT budget is managed at a departmental level. Resource allocation is roughly aligned to priorities. Capacity management is performed ad-hoc. No enterprise-level IT resource planning. |
| **Key Observations** | Duplicate tooling across acquisitions (3 different EHR platforms, 2 identity systems) indicates resource inefficiency. IT spend as % of revenue is estimated at 5.2%, above industry benchmark of 3.8%, partly due to redundancy. |

### EDM05 — Ensure Stakeholder Engagement

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|
| **Current Maturity** | **1** — Performed (Ad-hoc) |
| **Target Maturity** | **3** — Defined (Standardized) |
| **Gap** | **2** |
| **Evidence** | No IT steering committee exists. Stakeholder communication is informal and inconsistent. Business unit leaders report limited visibility into IT initiatives. No formal escalation mechanism for IT issues. |
| **Key Observations** | Two business units independently procured SaaS solutions without IT involvement in the past 6 months. Post-acquisition integration status is not communicated in a structured way. |

## 3. APO — Align, Plan and Organise (Management)

### APO01 — Manage the IT Management Framework

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|
| **Current Maturity** | **1** — Performed (Ad-hoc) |
| **Target Maturity** | **3** — Defined (Standardized) |
| **Gap** | **2** |
| **Evidence** | No enterprise IT management framework (ITIL, COBIT, ISO 20000). IT processes are undocumented and inconsistently applied across business units. No process ownership structure exists. |
| **Key Observations** | The acquired entities brought three different ITSM approaches. No consolidation or standardization effort has been initiated. Change management is performed differently in every unit. |

### APO02 — Manage Strategy

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|
| **Current Maturity** | **2** — Managed (Basic) |
| **Target Maturity** | **4** — Measured (Quantitative) |
| **Gap** | **2** |
| **Evidence** | An annual IT operating plan exists but is not formally linked to enterprise strategic planning. No multi-year IT strategic plan. Technology roadmaps are maintained in silos by each business unit. |
| **Key Observations** | The current IT plan is primarily operational (budget and headcount) rather than strategic. Digital transformation goals are stated but not quantified. No architecture-driven strategy. |

### APO03 — Manage Enterprise Architecture

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|
| **Current Maturity** | **1** — Performed (Ad-hoc) |
| **Target Maturity** | **3** — Defined (Standardized) |
| **Gap** | **2** |
| **Evidence** | No enterprise architecture function or framework (TOGAF, Zachman). System landscape documentation is incomplete and outdated. No technology standards or reference architecture. |
| **Key Observations** | Integration challenges between the telemedicine platform and acquired EHR systems are symptomatic of missing architecture governance. Point-to-point integrations are the norm. |

### APO07 — Manage Human Resources

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|
| **Current Maturity** | **2** — Managed (Basic) |
| **Target Maturity** | **3** — Defined (Standardized) |
| **Gap** | **1** |
| **Evidence** | IT roles and responsibilities are defined at the team level but not standardized enterprise-wide. Training is conducted informally without a structured program. No formal IT competency framework or career progression model. |
| **Key Observations** | Staff retention in IT is below industry average (12% voluntary turnover vs. 8% benchmark). Post-acquisition, key IT personnel from acquired entities left due to unclear career paths. |

### APO08 — Manage Relationships

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|
| **Current Maturity** | **1** — Performed (Ad-hoc) |
| **Target Maturity** | **3** — Defined (Standardized) |
| **Gap** | **2** |
| **Evidence** | No formal IT-business relationship management process. Business engagement is reactive and dependent on personal relationships. No service level agreements (SLAs) between IT and business units. |
| **Key Observations** | Business satisfaction with IT is low (surveyed at 3.1/5). Business units perceive IT as a bottleneck. No formal demand management or intake process for IT requests. |

### APO12 — Manage Risk

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|
| **Current Maturity** | **1** — Performed (Ad-hoc) |
| **Target Maturity** | **3** — Defined (Standardized) |
| **Gap** | **2** |
| **Evidence** | No formal IT risk management process. No risk register, no risk treatment plans, no regular risk assessment cycle. Cybersecurity risk is partially addressed but not as part of an enterprise risk framework. |
| **Key Observations** | Risk management is confused with cybersecurity. Operational, compliance, and strategic IT risks are not identified or assessed. No risk appetite statement exists for IT. |

### APO13 — Manage Security

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|
| **Current Maturity** | **2** — Managed (Basic) |
| **Target Maturity** | **4** — Measured (Quantitative) |
| **Gap** | **2** |
| **Evidence** | A basic information security program exists with a CISO and security team. Antivirus, firewall, and access controls are deployed. However, no ISO 27001-aligned ISMS, no formal security awareness program, and no security metrics dashboard. |
| **Key Observations** | Security policies are fragmented across acquired entities. Third-party security assessments are not performed. No formal incident response plan. Penetration testing is conducted ad-hoc. |

## 5. DSS — Deliver, Service and Support (Management)

### DSS01 — Manage Operations

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|
| **Current Maturity** | **2** — Managed (Basic) |
| **Target Maturity** | **3** — Defined (Standardized) |

### DSS05 — Manage Security Services

| Attribute | Rating |
|---|---|

| **Target Maturity** | **4** — Measured (Quantitative) |

### Key Findings

- **Weakest domain:** MEA (1.00) — Monitoring, evaluation, and assessment processes are essentially absent.
- **Strongest domain:** DSS (2.00) — Operational and security services have basic management practices.
- **Largest gap:** MEA domain (2.00 gap) — Requires most urgent attention for SOX readiness.
- **Critical mass:** 12 of 19 objectives are at Level 1 (Ad-hoc), indicating a systemic governance deficiency.
